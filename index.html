Conduct
Excessive running is a DQ. You may only run to help teammates.
FFA running
In FFA's, running is allowed — as long as it's not to hide. Treat an FFA like getting ganked in the Depths on a public server: if you're clearly not winning, run. Once the host sees the player count drop low enough, running is re-banned.

Section B·VII

General Rules

Knock Rule
If you are knocked, you are out. You may not get back up and rejoin the fight. (Does not apply in FFA's)
Redos — Overview

Redos are only called under the conditions in Section B·VIII. Once a guild calls one, the host's unbiased judgment (mods may chime in) decides its legitimacy by vote. Only then does a redo happen — and once it's played out, the decision can't be overturned.

Section B·VIII

Redos

When the round breaks, this is the only path back. Two checks, then two verdicts.

Calling a redo
Call it mid-match — notify the other team and the host.
Screen-record / clip whatever caused it (not required if you're being ganked).
Can't chat?
If your chat is off and you can't call it out, run from the fight and make obvious, odd movements to signal something's wrong. The host will reach out and pause the fight to sort it.

Miss either step → no redo. A fraudulent call can cost your team the round, at host discretion.

No call, no redo
If the round finishes and no rule break was called out during it, the result stands — there's no redo after the fact. Flag it mid-match or it's final.

◢ Redo CAN be called

  • Game itself crashes
  • Excessive running > 12s (painfully obvious; 50/50s default to no call)
  • An opponent is caught using a banned item / tech / misc
  • The match is interrupted by gankers

◣ Redo CANNOT be called

  • Getting sent into the air and taking damage (depends on damage taken and drop distance)
How a valid redo plays out
It doesn't restart the round — it resumes from where the round stood when it stopped.
Example: a 2v4 war gets interrupted by gankers. It restarts as a 2v4 with all players at 100% HP — not a clean reset.

Section B·IX

Depths Wagers

All unbans or changed wager conditions go in the wager ticket — never voice chat.
You must have Bell before wagering.

Section B·X

1v1 Event Rules

Everything's on the table
In a 1v1 event, all mantras, weapons, oaths, and enchants are UNBANNED. There are no set bans — the two players agree on what to ban for the fight.
Still off-limits
Techs, move stacks, bugs, and misc bans (campfires, etc.) stay banned during every event.
